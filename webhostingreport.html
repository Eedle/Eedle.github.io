Webserver
A webserver is a piece of physical hardware hosting one or more websites. Through software making use of the Hyper Text Transfer Protocol, a webserver can deliver the files that comprise said sites to other machines as requested through the World Wide Web, where they are then interpreted by the receiver’s http client.

Why You Need a Webserver

A webserver is necessary if you intend to host your website on the World Wide Web.

Why it Takes more Than a Domain Name to Host a Website

A domain name is what identifies a website, but it isn’t what hosts it. The acquisition of a domain name is similar to acquiring a license to host a website under that specific name, and does not provide the means to host it in of itself.

Common Webhosting Company Services

Standard Services provided by a webhosting company include:

  • Webhosting
  • An Email Account
  • FTPS Uploads

A Webhosting Company can provide the option for the owner of a domain name to create email addresses ending with said domain name as apposed to an extension of a webmail service provider such as gmail.com. FTTPS Access allows the user to upload files created locally on their machine to the hosted webserver

Some Webhosting services also include with their subscription tools that make it easier for beginners to build websites without relying on the use of html. To this end, the support of wordpress as a building tool is often included. Anyone can run wordpress software for free, but to put a site made with wordpress on the web a wordpress host is required. Wordpress hosts may also offer unique upgrades to the program, as wordpress’s code is open source.

What to look for When Selecting a Webhosting Company

Things to consider when searching for a website hosting company include the quality of their costumer services, the price of subscription, whether they’ll try to trick you into paying fees that aren’t immediately obvious, and the “uptime” of their server (how often the server hosting your website can be expected to run).

Uptime is often listed as a percent. It is difficult to independently verify, and companies often fail to list what the uptime is a percentage of. It you do the math, the difference between 90% of a month, day, or a week can result in a very different number of hours that your website will be available.

Other things to consider might include whether you want your website to have dedicated hosting or shared hosting. Hosting your website on a shared server means that website’s belonging to other users are also hosted on that server. Shared servers can translate to less responsibility for the site creator as they may have a support staff of technicians, but also allow for less control. Dedicated servers are servers dedicated to a single user, and offer things like full administrative privileges.

Average Price Range of Webhosting Companies

On average, hosting a single website on a shared server costs about 3.40 dollars per month.
